# GrowlerDB Trademark Policy

**GrowlerDB™** and the GrowlerDB logo are trademarks of **GrowlerDB LLC**. The *code* is open source
under [AGPL-3.0](LICENSE); this policy governs the *name and logo*, which the license does not grant.
The goal is the usual open-source balance: let the community talk about, build on, and redistribute
GrowlerDB freely, while preventing uses that confuse people about what is official.

## You may (no permission needed)

- Use the "GrowlerDB" name to **refer to the project** truthfully — in articles, talks, docs, and
  comparisons.
- Say your product is **"compatible with GrowlerDB,"** **"for GrowlerDB,"** or **"built on GrowlerDB,"**
  as long as it doesn't imply we endorse or produce it.
- **Redistribute unmodified** official builds under the name.
- Use the name in **community groups, meetups, and content** that are clearly unofficial.

## You may not (without written permission)

- Use "GrowlerDB" or the logo in a way that implies **official status, endorsement, or affiliation**
  when there is none.
- Name a **product, service, company, or domain** so as to be **confusingly similar** (e.g.
  "GrowlerDB Cloud", "GrowlerDB Enterprise", "getgrowlerdb.com").
- Apply the GrowlerDB name or logo to a **modified version or fork** — you're free to fork the AGPL
  code, but **give your fork a different name** so users aren't misled about its origin.
- Sell **merchandise** bearing the marks, or register the marks (or confusingly similar ones) anywhere.

## Modified versions & forks

The AGPL lets you modify and redistribute the code. When you do, **use your own name and branding** for
the result, and you may state factually that it is "derived from GrowlerDB." Don't present a fork as if
it were official GrowlerDB.

## Logo

Don't alter the logo's proportions, colors, or elements, and don't use it as your own app/product icon.

## Questions & permission

For uses not covered here, or to request permission, contact GrowlerDB LLC via the channels in
[`SUPPORT.md`](SUPPORT.md) / [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md). We aim to be permissive for community and
non-commercial use; this policy exists to protect users from confusion, not to restrict honest use.
